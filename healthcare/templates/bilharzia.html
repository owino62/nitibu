Bilharzia, also known as schistosomiasis, is a parasitic disease caused by trematode worms from the genus Schistosoma.

It is transmitted to humans through contact with contaminated water, where the larvae of the parasite, released by infected snails, penetrate the skin.

The parasites mature in the human bloodstream and can cause damage to organs such as the liver, intestines, bladder, and even the nervous system.
